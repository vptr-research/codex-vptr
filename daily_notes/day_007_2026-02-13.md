## What I Learned

### New Tricks
- `!*str` is same as `*str == '\0'` (zero = false in C)
- String literals (`char *str = "hello"`) cannot be modified
- Use `char str[] = "hello"` when you need to modify

### ASCII Ranges
- Numbers: 48-57
- Uppercase: 65-90
- Lowercase: 97-122
- Separators: everything else (not alphanumeric)

### ft_strcapitalize Logic
- Word = sequence of alphanumeric characters (letters + numbers)
- Separator = anything that is NOT a letter or number
- Capitalize first letter of each word
- Lowercase the rest
- Numbers count as part of the word (42palavras = one word)

## Hardest Exercise
ft_strcapitalize took many attempts because:
- Easy to mix up str[i] and str[i-1] in conditions
- Needed to understand what counts as a "separator"
- Numbers are NOT separators (part of the word)
